Spark Repair Estimator

1. Most Interesting Design / UX Decision

The most important UX decision was moving the running total and progress into a persistent bottom ledger bar. The user is not sitting at a desk; they are walking through a distressed property, often with one thumb, bad lighting, and no signal. The ledger bar keeps the only number that must never be lost in the thumb zone, while item rows stay dense: full item name, price/unit, quantity, and line total. I also made room management explicit with an Edit control instead of hiding destructive actions behind gestures.

2. What Is Broken or Fragile

The most fragile area is install behavior because mobile browsers handle PWAs differently. Android Chrome can expose a native install prompt, while iOS Safari requires Share -> Add to Home Screen, so the app provides fallback install instructions when the prompt is unavailable. OCR is also fragile by nature: it runs offline and never invents serials, but accuracy depends on photo quality, glare, focus, and whether Tesseract assets were cached after first load. Photo storage is bounded by localStorage quota; images are downscaled to 1280px JPEGs, and if the quota is still exceeded the app saves the estimate data without the photos and tells the user rather than failing silently.

3. Creative Addition

The creative addition is the offline Field Assistant and review engine: a chat over the live estimate that is deterministic, not an LLM — a scored keyword-intent engine reading only the project state and the embedded price list. It flags likely missed costs before export, builds a copyable contractor scope summary, aggregates materials by unit, answers price and progress questions with one-turn follow-ups ("and the kitchen?"), defines field terms from a built-in glossary ("what does plank mean?"), and attaches chips to every answer that deep-link into the exact room and group. I chose this because the biggest field risk is not only slow entry; it is forgetting expensive scope before the acquisition decision is made.

4. What I Would Ship Next With Two More Days

With two more days, I would add stronger two-tab conflict handling, a dedicated export progress view for very large photo packages, and a guided first-run sample walkthrough. I would also improve photo tagging so serial plates and damage shots can be tied to room, group, and item with fewer taps.

AI Tool Role

AI tools were used as a development assistant for implementation, debugging, UI polish, acceptance-test planning, and regression checks. Final product decisions, manual device testing, and submission review were performed by me.
